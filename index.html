cylinder head porting

Porting a cylinder head is a popular DIY process. The procedure involves enlarging the ports of the cylinder head. It is a time-consuming procedure that requires precise scribing and removing the gasket. The ports are then gradually opened to match the gasket. In contrast, reshaping the cylinder head is not necessary. Rather, the porting process creates a better-performing engine and improves its performance.

Before cylinder head porting, it is essential to make sure the cylinder heads are properly gasket matched and that the intake and exhaust ports are smooth. Intake ports should be smooth, while exhaust ports should be shiny and free of grits. An 80-grit finish is best, because anything higher will disturb turbulence, which is essential for proper fuel atomization. In addition, rough casting of the port runners can be easily eliminated using an abrasive roll.

Cylinder heads are often referred to as the lungs of the engine, because they draw air into the cylinder, utilize it to create energy, and then expel it. Standard cylinder heads are like a smoker's lungs, containing narrow airways that limit airflow. However, ported shims reduce the restriction of the airways and improve performance.
